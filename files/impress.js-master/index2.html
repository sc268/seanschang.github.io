Random Forest

Methods
  • ▪ Train each tree with bootstrapped data
  • ▪ For each split, only m randomly selected variables will be considered
  • ▪ Make votes to aggregate results from different trees

Motivation
  • ▪ Bootstrapping and aggregating: reduce variance
  • ▪ Randomly choose variables: prevent overfitting

Pros
  • ▪ Accuracy
  • ▪ Lower variance

Cons
  • ▪ Interpretation
  • ▪ Slower

log billing of Cardiology insurance providers
Algorithms

1. For each speciality, compute its 95% percentile.
2. Use aforementioned random forest classification to reclassify each insurance provider.
3. Evaluate each provider's billing amount in new speciality. Report it if the amount exceeds the threshold.

Applications

Speciality classification
  • ▪ The random Forest classifier improves the speciality classification accuracy and scalability.

  • ▪ Peer group comparision have more reliable thresholds for those providers whose reported and assigned specialties are same.

  • ▪ Applied to other conditions such as SEEG_EEG (procedure code 95957)to identify who billed EEG digital analysis more frequently or paid more than peers’ average.


Percentile comparison
  • ▪ Use 95% as the threshold, test on CTNFL and CTNOH, the algorithm was able to identify 55 out of the 119 triggered providers from CTNFL

  • ▪ In addition, 5 out of 57 triggered providers from CTNOH
