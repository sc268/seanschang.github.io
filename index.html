Brief Bio

Sean Chang is a Ph.D. candidate in Statistical Science at Duke University under the supervision of professor James Berger. He has been working at Facebook, Goldman Sachs and Verisk Analytics during summers. His interests lie in the area of machine learning and Bayesian statistics. Co-authored with his advisor, they have established Bayes and Empirical Bayes procedures on false positive probability of mutually exclusive hypotheses under the scenario of data dependence. He received his M.A. and B.Sc., both in mathematics, from Duke and National Taiwan University, respectively. Chang was on the Dean's list in college and his team in the Triangle Healthcare Challenge was the winner of both the Grand Prize and the Validic mHealth prize.
