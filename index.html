Brief Bio

Sean Chang is a Ph.D. candidate in Statistical Science at Duke University under the supervision of professor James Berger. He has been working at Goldman Sachs and Verisk Analytics as a summer associate and data scientist intern. His interests lie in the area of machine learning and Bayesian statistics, including online advertising, large-scale inference in eQTL and multiple testing on both correlated data and clinical trials. Co-authored with his advisor, they have established Bayes and Empirical Bayes procedures on false positive probability of mutually exclusive hypotheses under the scenario of data dependence. He received his M.A. and B.Sc., both in mathematics, from Duke and National Taiwan University, respectively. Chang was on the awarded Dean's list in college and receive the Study Abroad Scholarship from the Ministry of Education, Taiwan.
