Brief Bio

Shih-Han Sean Chang is a doctoral student with an emphasis in Bayesian Statistics working with
Prof. Jim Berger. He received his B.S., M.A. in Mathematics from National Taiwan University and Duke University, respectively. Sean is currently involved in research concerning false discovery rate in multiple testing, which becomes increasingly vital in many data driven problems such as gene expressions. His interests include Monte Carlo method and Large-scale inference as well.
